Featured project

I automated a tax law firm's client pipeline, end to end, as a non-engineer.

Brotman Law had no dedicated intake specialist, so I took intake on. Then I built an AI operations suite that runs a potential client from the first phone call through retainers, powers of attorney, and onboarding, with the firm's real rules and guardrails encoded. Four reusable skills, four connected systems, zero invented legal language.

Four skills, each triggered by a phrase

Intake · pre-consult

Intake

Runs a potential new client from first contact to a booked, paid consult.

  • Finds the contact in the CRM, including under a relative or business entity
  • Hands over a discovery-first call script with probing follow-ups
  • Logs the Fireflies callback as a proper CRM note and moves the deal stage
  • Books against the attorney's real calendar; runs the refer-out protocol when it isn't a fit

Onboarding · post-consult

Onboarding

Takes a client from the end of the consult to an opened matter.

  • Pulls the full consult transcript, including the wrap-up dictated after the client leaves
  • Drafts retainers from current firm templates only (Flat Fee, Hourly, Submarine)
  • Attaches the matching power of attorney per agency, with the right signing rules
  • Preps Clio onboarding, the secure e-folder, and the internal New Matter email

Coverage · live call

Intake Script Generator

Builds a verbatim, read-aloud script so anyone can cover the phones and sound trained.

  • Cold mode: full universal spine plus a trigger table to jump to the right matter block
  • Tailored mode: confirms what's known, asks only what's missing, leads with the deadline

Email Responder

Drafts client and internal replies in my voice. Never sends. I read and send every one.

What it connects, and what it can't

HubSpot · CRM, contacts, deals, pipeline
Fireflies · call and consult transcripts
Microsoft 365 · Outlook calendar and email
Calendly · consult booking
Clio · no connector → driven via browser
Dropbox Sign · no connector → manual prep

The systems with no integration are routed through Claude in Chrome and structured manual prep instead of being faked. Knowing the boundary up front is what keeps the whole thing honest.

The hard part was encoding the mess

Data lives where systems put itIntake details land in the CRM phone field via a Zapier rule, not the notes, so that is where the system reads them.
Calendar holds lieHolds are routinely marked "free" instead of "busy," so every slot is verified against the real Outlook calendar before it is confirmed.
The decision is at the endThe attorney dictates the consult wrap-up after the client leaves, so billing, scope, and PoA decisions live at the end of the transcript.
Records hide under other namesA deal may be filed under a relative or a business entity, so a lookup checks family members and entities too.

Anyone can connect a CRM. Knowing to read the phone field is the job.

The guardrails are the product

Not the attorney. No legal or tax advice, no outcome predictions. It relays the attorney's guidance and routes merits questions to the consult.
Approval before every write. Nothing is written to the CRM or sent to a client without my sign-off.
Client PII never touches the AI. Social Security and EIN numbers go on the form by my hand only, never into notes, memory, or chat.
Confidentiality holds. Transcript links are never pasted into the CRM; records are referenced by title and date.

It runs on a schedule, not just on demand

The last move was the one that made it a system instead of a pile of one-off conversations. Two scheduled tasks run on their own each day, one to open it and one to close it, each reading straight from the system where the answer actually lives.

Morning · scheduled
PNC brief
A start-of-day task that looks through Fireflies, reading the notes and transcripts from the potential new client consults and callbacks, and tells me which PNCs need attention and what the next step is.
End of day · scheduled
Intake count
A close-out task that looks through my Outlook calendar to tally the day's intake activity, so the pipeline is reconciled before I log off.

What this proves

I took a real, messy, high-stakes professional pipeline and automated it end to end across CRM, scheduling, transcripts, documents, and email, encoding the firm's actual rules and guardrails, as a non-engineer, by understanding the work deeply enough to translate it into software. Then I packaged it into reusable, trigger-phrase workflows and a daily operating rhythm, so the process runs the same way every time, with or without me at the desk.
